The Louisiana VRA dashboard allows you to explore districts in terms of their history of offering effective opportunity to Black voters to elect candidates of choice. We have pre-computed some racially polarized voting statistics in order to facilitate this exploration. In particular, we use ecological inference (EI) to identify the Black candidates of choice in statewide elections from 2015-2019. Our EI runs are done at the statewide level using eiPack (a Bayesian hierarchical model for RxC inference). The confidence in correctly identifying the candidate of choice depends on the number of times out of 1000 draws that the candidate had the most Black votes. In this case, we got 1000/1000 agreement for each identified candidate of choice in both the primary and the general/runoff. We also weight more recent elections more heavily, and we put twice as much weight on elections in which the candidate of choice is themselves Black-identified. This is in keeping with the way VRA attorneys treat some elections as more probative (providing more persuasive evidence). For details about the construction of the “effectiveness score,” see LINK.

When you’re drawing maps in the module, you can see the electoral history of the district as you draw by going to the VRA tab and selecting the district under “District details.” We have colored successful primary elections green. Success for the Black candidate of choice is assessed in a primary when that candidate either (a) receives a majority of votes in the district you drew in that primary, or (b) comes in first or second in the primary, but no candidate receives a majority.
